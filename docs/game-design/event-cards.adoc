= Event and Crisis Cards
:toc:
:icons: font

== Overview

Crisis and event cards introduce dynamic disruptions each turn. Cards are drawn
from era-specific decks, creating escalating complexity as the game progresses.

== Card Anatomy

Each card has:

* *Title* — evocative name
* *Era* — which deck it belongs to (or "Any")
* *Type* — Environmental, Social, Technological, Economic, Geopolitical, or Positive
* *Effect* — immediate mechanical impact
* *Choice* — player decision (where applicable)
* *Cascade* — secondary boundary effects
* *Historical Note* — real-world parallel for educational value

== Agrarian Era Cards

=== The Great Flood
[source]
----
Type: Environmental
Effect: All coastal settlements lose 1 Food for 2 turns
Choice: Relocate inland (-1 Territory) OR build levees (-2 Capital)
Cascade: +1 Freshwater Change stress
Historical Note: Mesopotamian floods, Nile delta management
----

=== Plague
[source]
----
Type: Social
Effect: -2 Labour for 3 turns
Choice: Quarantine (-1 Trade) OR pray (50% chance of +1 turn duration)
Cascade: None
Historical Note: Black Death, Justinian Plague
----

=== Bountiful Harvest (Positive)
[source]
----
Type: Environmental (Positive)
Effect: +2 Food, +1 Nature for all players
Choice: Store surplus (+1 Capital next turn) OR share (+1 Stability for all)
Historical Note: Medieval warm period, agricultural surpluses
----

== Industrial Era Cards

=== Industrial Revolution
[source]
----
Type: Economic
Effect: +2 Capital, +2 Climate Change stress
Choice: Ignore (keep Capital) OR invest in green tech (-1 Capital, -1 stress)
Cascade: +1 Atmospheric Aerosol Loading
Historical Note: British Industrial Revolution, Luddite movement
----

=== Workers' Revolt
[source]
----
Type: Social
Effect: All factories shut down for 1 turn unless Labour >= 5
Choice: Negotiate (+1 Labour cost permanently) OR suppress (-2 Stability)
Cascade: None
Historical Note: Chartist movement, Paris Commune, labour unions
----

=== Colonial Independence
[source]
----
Type: Geopolitical
Effect: Player with most colonies loses 1 colony
Choice: Grant independence (+2 Stability) OR resist (-1 Labour, -1 Stability)
Cascade: None
Historical Note: American Revolution, Indian independence, decolonisation
----

=== Smog Crisis
[source]
----
Type: Environmental
Effect: -1 Labour (health), +2 Atmospheric Aerosol stress
Choice: Clean Air Act (-1 Capital, -2 Aerosol stress) OR ignore
Cascade: +1 Climate Change stress
Historical Note: London Great Smog 1952, Clean Air Acts
----

== Digital Era Cards

=== AI Uprising
[source]
----
Type: Technological
Effect: All automated production halts for 2 turns
Resolution: Negotiate (spend 3 Knowledge) OR suppress (spend 2 Labour + 2 Capital)
Cascade: -1 Stability for all players with automation > 3
Historical Note: Speculative — AI alignment problem, automation anxiety
----

=== Cybersecurity Breach
[source]
----
Type: Technological
Effect: Target player loses 2 Capital (data theft)
Choice: Invest in security (-1 Capital, immunity for 3 turns) OR risk it
Cascade: None
Historical Note: Equifax breach, SolarWinds, state-sponsored hacking
----

=== Social Media Disinformation
[source]
----
Type: Social
Effect: -1 Stability for all players; +1 to any boundary stress (chosen by instigator)
Choice: Regulate platforms (-1 Knowledge) OR ride the wave (+1 Capital, -1 Stability)
Cascade: Chosen boundary +1
Historical Note: 2016 elections, anti-vax movements, climate denial
----

=== Renewable Energy Breakthrough (Positive)
[source]
----
Type: Technological (Positive)
Effect: -2 Climate Change stress for player who invested in green tech
Choice: Share technology (+1 Stability for all, -1 Climate for all) OR patent (+2 Capital)
Cascade: If shared, -1 Atmospheric Aerosol for all
Historical Note: Solar cost curve, wind power expansion
----

== Post-Scarcity Era Cards

=== Fusion Ignition
[source]
----
Type: Technological (Positive)
Effect: Energy becomes unlimited for one player; -3 Climate Change stress
Choice: Open-source (-2 Climate for all) OR monopolise (+3 Capital, +1 Novel Entities)
Cascade: If open-sourced, accelerates Dyson Sphere construction
Historical Note: ITER, NIF, fusion research
----

=== Space Debris Crisis
[source]
----
Type: Environmental
Effect: All space infrastructure offline for 1 turn; +2 Novel Entities stress
Choice: Clean up (-2 Capital) OR wait (10% chance of cascade each turn)
Cascade: Risk of Kessler Syndrome (permanent space access penalty)
Historical Note: Growing orbital debris problem, Kessler syndrome theory
----

=== Post-Human Split
[source]
----
Type: Social
Effect: Players must choose: augment (+2 Knowledge, -1 Stability) or remain (-1 Knowledge, +1 Stability)
Choice: Defines late-game strategy
Cascade: Social division between augmented and unaugmented factions
Historical Note: Transhumanism debates, genetic engineering ethics
----

=== Climate Tipping Point
[source]
----
Type: Environmental
Effect: ALL players lose 1 Food/turn until collective CO2 reduced below threshold
Choice: Emergency geoengineering (-3 Capital, -2 Climate stress, +2 Novel Entities) OR cooperate on reduction
Cascade: +1 Freshwater stress, +1 Ocean Acidification stress
Historical Note: IPCC tipping point warnings, Arctic methane, AMOC collapse risk
----

== Any-Era Cards

=== Montreal Protocol (Positive)
[source]
----
Type: Geopolitical (Positive)
Effect: Ozone boundary stress reduced to 0
Condition: Only if 3+ players agree to ban CFCs
Lesson: International cooperation works — bonus to all contributing players
Historical Note: Montreal Protocol 1987 — most successful environmental treaty
----

=== Tragedy of the Commons
[source]
----
Type: Environmental
Effect: Shared resource (fishery, forest) depleted; -1 Nature for all
Choice: Each player secretly decides: conserve or exploit (Prisoner's Dilemma)
Cascade: If majority exploit, -2 Nature and +1 Biosphere stress
Historical Note: Hardin (1968), overfishing, deforestation
----
