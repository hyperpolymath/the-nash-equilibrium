= Economic Systems and Pathways

== Overview

The Nash Equilibrium models four principal economic systems as victory pathways.
Each has distinct historical contexts, real-world examples, mechanical
implications, and relationships to planetary boundaries.

== The Four Pathways

=== 1. Capitalism

*Core Principles:* Private ownership, market competition, profit motive

*Historical Context:*

* Mercantilism and colonial expansion (15th-18th century)
* Industrial Revolution (18th-19th century)
* Modern global markets, financialisation, neoliberalism

*Real-World Examples:*

* British Industrial Revolution — rapid growth, massive pollution, inequality
* Post-WWII American consumer capitalism
* Modern tech industry — innovation paired with platform monopoly

*Game Mechanics:*

* Highest Capital generation rate
* +2 Gold/turn from trade routes
* Pollution generated per factory (Climate Change stress +1)
* Inequality mechanic: Labour unrest at high Capital/low Labour ratio
* Victory path: Industrialise -> Globalise -> Adapt (or collapse)

*Planetary Boundary Risk:* HIGH — accelerates Climate Change, Land System Change,
Novel Entities, Biogeochemical Flows

=== 2. Socialism

*Core Principles:* Collective ownership, planned economy, social welfare

*Historical Context:*

* Utopian socialism (Owen, Fourier, Saint-Simon)
* Marxist theory and Soviet implementation
* Social democracy (Scandinavian model)
* Cuban revolution and planned economy

*Real-World Examples:*

* Soviet Union — rapid industrialisation, space programme, environmental neglect
* Cuba — healthcare and education achievements, economic constraints
* Scandinavian social democracies — high welfare, market elements

*Game Mechanics:*

* +1 Stability/turn (social cohesion)
* Reduced inequality penalties
* Slower Capital generation
* Planned economy: choose resource allocation each era
* No market-driven innovation bonus (Knowledge generation slower)
* Victory path: Equality -> Stability -> Collective advancement

*Planetary Boundary Risk:* MODERATE — can either protect or neglect boundaries
depending on planning priorities (Soviet model: heavy industry, high pollution;
Scandinavian model: strong environmental protection)

=== 3. Green Economics

*Core Principles:* Sustainability, circular economy, well-being over GDP

*Historical Context:*

* Environmental movement (1960s-present)
* Doughnut Economics (Kate Raworth, 2017)
* Degrowth movement
* Sustainable Development Goals (UN, 2015)

*Real-World Examples:*

* Germany's Energiewende — energy transition to renewables
* Costa Rica — 99% renewable electricity, reforestation
* Bhutan's Gross National Happiness index
* Amsterdam's adoption of Doughnut Economics

*Game Mechanics:*

* +1 Nature/turn (conservation bonus)
* Planetary boundary stress reduction: -1 per turn on one chosen boundary
* Low Capital start; slower growth curve
* Circular economy: waste from one process feeds another (resource efficiency)
* Vulnerable to exploitation by Capitalist players in early eras
* Victory path: Balance -> Resilience -> Post-Scarcity (sustainable)

*Planetary Boundary Risk:* LOW — actively reduces boundary stress, but
vulnerable to being outpaced by aggressive players

=== 4. Techno-Utopian Economics

*Core Principles:* Technology solves scarcity, automation liberates, abundance
through innovation

*Historical Context:*

* Soviet utopian visions (Bogdanov's _Red Star_)
* Post-war techno-optimism (nuclear power, space age)
* Silicon Valley ideology (disruption, exponential growth)
* Fully Automated Luxury Communism (Bastani, 2019)

*Real-World Examples:*

* Apollo programme — state-directed technological achievement
* Internet revolution — information abundance (partial post-scarcity)
* Renewable energy cost curves — solar/wind approaching zero marginal cost
* Speculative: fusion power, space mining, strong AI

*Game Mechanics:*

* +1 Knowledge/turn (innovation bonus)
* High Knowledge cost for breakthroughs
* Unstable Labour (automation displaces workers)
* Breakthrough cards: powerful one-time effects (fusion, space elevator, etc.)
* Risk of AI revolt if Knowledge >> Labour
* Victory path: Automate -> Breakthrough -> Space (Dyson Sphere)

*Planetary Boundary Risk:* VARIABLE — can either solve boundaries through
technology or create new ones (Novel Entities, Energy demands)

== Pathway Interactions

Players on different pathways interact through the *eXchange* pillar:

* Capitalist/Green tension: trade vs. regulation
* Socialist/Techno-Utopian alliance: collective investment in breakthrough tech
* Capitalist/Techno-Utopian: innovation race with inequality risk
* Green/Socialist: social welfare + environmental protection coalition

These interactions create the *Nash Equilibrium* dynamic — players must find
optimal strategies given what others are doing. Pure domination rarely wins;
cooperation and strategic adaptation are essential.
