= Player Roles and Asymmetric Gameplay
:toc:
:icons: font

== Overview

Players assume asymmetric roles with unique starting bonuses, constraints, and
victory paths. This encourages diverse strategies and highlights tensions between
different economic philosophies.

== Role Cards

=== Queen Isabella

*Archetype:* Colonial Capitalist

[cols="1,3"]
|===
| Starting Bonuses | +2 Gold/turn (colonial trade routes)
| Constraints | Cannot use barter economy; high base pollution rate
| Starting Era Bonus | Strong in Industrial era (colonial wealth)
| Weakness | Vulnerable to independence movements and climate crises
| Path to Victory | Exploit -> Industrialise -> Adapt (or face revolution)
|===

*Flavour:* Represents the colonial-capitalist model — rapid wealth extraction
from new territories at the cost of indigenous populations and environmental
degradation. Must transition to sustainable practices before boundaries collapse.

*Historical parallel:* Spanish Empire, British East India Company, extraction
economies.

=== Gandhi

*Archetype:* Cooperative Socialist

[cols="1,3"]
|===
| Starting Bonuses | +1 Stability/turn (nonviolent resistance, social cohesion)
| Constraints | Cannot build military units; growth rate halved
| Starting Era Bonus | Strong in Agrarian era (community-based)
| Weakness | Slow expansion; vulnerable to military-capable players
| Path to Victory | Sustainability -> Equality -> Collective advancement
|===

*Flavour:* Represents non-violent, community-centred development. Strong social
fabric but slow economic growth. Wins through collective well-being rather than
individual dominance.

*Historical parallel:* Indian independence movement, cooperative movements,
Scandinavian social democracy.

=== Elon Musk

*Archetype:* Techno-Utopian Innovator

[cols="1,3"]
|===
| Starting Bonuses | +1 Knowledge/turn (innovation engine)
| Constraints | High Knowledge cost for breakthroughs; Labour instability
| Starting Era Bonus | Strong in Digital era (tech disruption)
| Weakness | Social instability from automation; expensive research
| Path to Victory | Automate -> Breakthrough -> Space (Dyson Sphere)
|===

*Flavour:* Represents the Silicon Valley vision — technology as the solution to
all problems. Powerful innovation engine but creates social disruption through
automation and can neglect environmental boundaries in pursuit of breakthroughs.

*Historical parallel:* Tech industry, space programmes, nuclear power optimism.

=== Green Activist

*Archetype:* Environmental Steward

[cols="1,3"]
|===
| Starting Bonuses | +1 Nature/turn (conservation, ecosystem restoration)
| Constraints | Low starting Capital; vulnerable to economic exploitation
| Starting Era Bonus | Weak early, strong in Post-Scarcity era
| Weakness | Economic disadvantage in Industrial/Digital eras
| Path to Victory | Balance -> Resilience -> Post-Scarcity (sustainable)
|===

*Flavour:* Represents the environmental movement and Green economics. Protects
planetary boundaries but starts economically weak. Becomes powerful in later eras
as boundary stress makes sustainability essential.

*Historical parallel:* Greenpeace, Costa Rica, doughnut economics, degrowth.

== Expansion Roles (Future)

Potential additional roles for expansion packs:

* *Pharaoh* — Agrarian era specialist, monumental construction
* *Marx* — Revolutionary Socialist, can trigger regime change in other players
* *Pirate Queen* — Disrupts trade routes, asymmetric warfare
* *Indigenous Elder* — Deep Nature connection, unique boundary recovery abilities
* *AI Singleton* — Digital era specialist, post-human victory path

== Role Balance Notes

* Roles are intentionally *not* balanced for raw power — they are balanced for
  *interesting decisions*
* Each role has a window of strength and vulnerability
* The Nash Equilibrium dynamic means no single role can win alone in most games
* Playtesting should focus on ensuring each role has meaningful agency in every era
