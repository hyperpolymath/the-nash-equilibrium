= Core Game Mechanics
:toc:
:icons: font

== Overview

The Nash Equilibrium combines resource management, era progression, crisis
events, and game-theoretic player interaction into a coherent 6X experience.

== Resources

Players manage five key resources, each tied to planetary boundaries:

[cols="1,2,2"]
|===
| Resource | Description | Boundary Links

| *Labour*
| Workforce, human capital, social stability
| Freshwater Change (water for agriculture/industry)

| *Capital*
| Wealth, infrastructure, industrial capacity
| Climate Change (CO2), Novel Entities (pollution)

| *Nature*
| Biodiversity, ecosystem services, raw materials
| Biosphere Integrity, Land System Change

| *Knowledge*
| Science, technology, cultural advancement
| Stratospheric Ozone (CFC discovery/ban), Novel Entities

| *Energy*
| Power generation, fuel, thermodynamic capacity
| Climate Change (fossil fuels), Atmospheric Aerosol Loading
|===

=== Resource Flow

* Resources are generated per turn based on infrastructure, pathway, and era
* Resources are spent to build, research, trade, and recover boundaries
* Resource scarcity triggers social unrest (Labour), environmental degradation
  (Nature), or stagnation (Knowledge)

== Era Progression

The game progresses through four eras. Era transitions are triggered by
collective player progress (not individual).

=== Era 1: Agrarian (Turns 1-8)

* *Economy:* Barter, subsistence agriculture, early trade
* *Boundaries:* Low stress; Nature is abundant
* *Mechanics:* Simple resource collection; land claiming (eXplore, eXpand)
* *Key decisions:* Where to settle, what to cultivate, first trade agreements

=== Era 2: Industrial (Turns 9-16)

* *Economy:* Factories, mass production, colonial expansion
* *Boundaries:* Rapid stress increase on Climate, Land, Biogeochemical
* *Mechanics:* Factory building, pollution tracking, inequality mechanics
* *Key decisions:* Growth rate vs. sustainability, worker rights, imperialism
* *New mechanic:* Pollution tokens placed on planetary boundary tracks

=== Era 3: Digital (Turns 17-24)

* *Economy:* Automation, information technology, global networks
* *Boundaries:* Novel Entities emerge; all boundaries under pressure
* *Mechanics:* AI research, automation (Labour displacement), digital commons
* *Key decisions:* AI governance, data ownership, platform power
* *New mechanic:* Breakthrough cards available; AI revolt risk

=== Era 4: Post-Scarcity (Turns 25-32)

* *Economy:* Potential abundance or collapse
* *Boundaries:* Recovery possible or cascade failure
* *Mechanics:* Megaprojects (Dyson Sphere, space elevator), boundary recovery
* *Key decisions:* Cooperate for collective victory or compete for dominance
* *New mechanic:* Megaproject construction (requires multi-player cooperation)

== Victory Conditions

=== Cooperative Victory: Post-Scarcity Utopia

* Construct a Dyson Sphere (or equivalent megaproject)
* All planetary boundaries in Green or Amber zone
* No player in social collapse
* *Requires:* Multi-player cooperation through eXchange pillar

=== Pathway Victories (Individual)

[cols="1,3,2"]
|===
| Pathway | Condition | Difficulty

| *Capitalist*
| Highest Capital + active trade routes to all players
| Medium (boundary risk)

| *Socialist*
| Highest Stability + all players above poverty threshold
| Hard (slow growth)

| *Green*
| All boundaries in Green zone + Nature above starting level
| Hard (requires cooperation)

| *Techno-Utopian*
| 3+ breakthrough technologies + Dyson Sphere under construction
| Very Hard (high cost)
|===

=== Loss Conditions

* *Planetary Collapse:* 4+ boundaries at Black (stress 10) — ALL players lose
* *Social Collapse:* Labour drops to 0 — that player is eliminated
* *Resource Exhaustion:* Nature drops to 0 — cascading boundary failures begin

== Crisis Events

Crisis cards are drawn each turn from a deck scaled to the current era.

=== Crisis Card Categories

[cols="1,1,3"]
|===
| Category | Era Weight | Examples

| *Environmental*
| All eras (increasing)
| Climate tipping point, mass extinction, ocean dead zones

| *Social*
| Industrial+
| Workers' revolt, refugee crisis, inequality uprising

| *Technological*
| Digital+
| AI uprising, cybersecurity breach, automation displacement

| *Economic*
| Industrial+
| Market crash, hyperinflation, trade war

| *Geopolitical*
| All eras
| War declaration, sanctions, alliance fracture
|===

== Turn Structure

. *Resource Phase:* Collect resources based on infrastructure and pathway
. *Action Phase:* Build, research, trade, expand (2-3 actions per turn)
. *Exchange Phase:* Negotiate trades, alliances, agreements (eXchange pillar)
. *Experiment Phase:* Invest in research; resolve breakthrough attempts (eXperiment pillar)
. *Crisis Phase:* Draw and resolve crisis card
. *Boundary Phase:* Update planetary boundary dashboard; resolve cascades
. *Assessment Phase:* Check victory/loss conditions

== The Nash Equilibrium Mechanic

The game's namesake mechanic operates at the meta-strategic level:

* Players must find *equilibrium strategies* — optimal responses given what
  others are doing
* Pure exploitation (Capitalist without cooperation) leads to boundary collapse
* Pure cooperation without competition leads to stagnation
* The *winning position* is the Nash Equilibrium: no player can improve their
  outcome by unilaterally changing strategy

This is reinforced mechanically through:

* Trade agreements that benefit both parties but create dependency
* Crisis events that punish non-cooperative behaviour
* Victory conditions that reward balanced approaches
* The planetary boundary dashboard as a shared constraint
