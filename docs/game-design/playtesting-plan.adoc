= Playtesting Plan
:toc:
:icons: font

== Overview

Structured playtesting to validate core mechanics, balance, educational value,
and fun factor before Kickstarter launch.

== Phase 1: Paper Prototype (Weeks 1-2)

=== Setup

* Index cards for crisis/event cards
* Tokens (coins, cubes, or counters) for resources
* Hand-drawn hex map on large paper/whiteboard
* Printed planetary boundaries dashboard (A3)
* Printed player boards and role cards

=== Focus Areas

* Does the resource flow make sense?
* Is the planetary boundaries dashboard readable and impactful?
* Are era transitions meaningful?
* Is the turn structure smooth (7 phases not too many)?
* Do crisis cards create interesting decisions?

=== Test Scenarios

[cols="1,3,2"]
|===
| Scenario | Setup | Tests

| *Capitalist Runaway*
| All players choose Capitalist pathway
| Does boundary collapse happen too fast/slow? Is there meaningful choice?

| *Green vs. Capitalist*
| 2 Green, 2 Capitalist
| Is the Green pathway viable? Does eXchange create interesting tension?

| *Cooperation Test*
| All players attempt cooperative victory (Dyson Sphere)
| Is cooperative path achievable? Is it fun? Do free-riders get punished?

| *Speed Run*
| Techno-Utopian rush to Post-Scarcity
| Is the tech rush balanced? Can it be stopped? Is it fun to play against?

| *Full Asymmetric*
| One of each role (Isabella, Gandhi, Musk, Green Activist)
| Do all roles feel viable? Do they create interesting interactions?
|===

=== Participants

* 5-10 people per session
* Mix of board game hobbyists and non-gamers
* At least one session with economics students
* At least one session with younger players (12-16)

=== Feedback Collection

* Post-session questionnaire (5-point Likert + free text)
* In-game observation notes (decision points, confusion, engagement)
* Record game length and boundary stress trajectories
* Track which victory conditions are achieved/attempted

== Phase 2: Digital Prototype — Tabletop Simulator (Weeks 3-6)

=== Implementation

* Custom card decks with proper layout
* Scripted planetary boundaries dashboard
* Automated turn phase progression
* Resource counter objects
* Hex map with snap-to-grid

=== Focus Areas

* Does the game work remotely?
* Are rules clear enough for self-directed play?
* Balance tuning with larger sample size
* Edge case discovery (rules interactions, degenerate strategies)

=== Distribution

* Share on BoardGameGeek (design diary + playtest invitation)
* Reddit: r/tabletopgamedesign, r/boardgames
* Direct invitations to experienced playtesters
* Target: 20-50 external playtesters

=== Metrics

* Game length (target: 90-120 minutes for full 4-era game)
* Boundary stress trajectories (should accelerate through eras)
* Victory distribution (no single pathway should dominate)
* Player engagement (which eras feel slow/exciting?)
* Dropout rate (do players want to finish?)

== Phase 3: Balance Iteration (Weeks 7-10)

=== Key Balance Questions

. Is the Capitalist pathway too strong in early eras?
. Does the Green pathway have enough agency before Post-Scarcity?
. Are crisis cards too random / too deterministic?
. Is the cascade mechanic (boundary stress spillover) tuned correctly?
. Is 32 turns the right game length?
. Is the cooperative victory achievable but not trivial?

=== Tuning Levers

* Resource generation rates per pathway
* Boundary stress accumulation rates
* Crisis card distribution across eras
* Cascade thresholds and rates
* Victory condition thresholds
* Action count per turn (2 vs. 3)
* Era length (8 turns each, or variable?)

== Phase 4: Educational Validation (Weeks 9-12)

=== Classroom Testing

* Partner with 2-3 economics/environmental science teachers
* Test with actual student groups (secondary and university)
* Measure learning outcomes before and after play
* Assess whether game creates discussion and engagement

=== Questions

* Do students understand planetary boundaries after playing?
* Can they explain the Prisoner's Dilemma / Nash Equilibrium?
* Does the game prompt discussion about real-world parallels?
* Is the complexity appropriate for the target age range?
* Do educators see value in integrating it into curriculum?

== Success Criteria

Before proceeding to Kickstarter:

* [ ] Average game length within 90-150 minutes
* [ ] All four pathways achieve victory in at least 20% of games
* [ ] Planetary collapse occurs in 10-30% of games (not too rare, not too common)
* [ ] Player satisfaction rating >= 4/5
* [ ] At least 2 educators endorse for classroom use
* [ ] No degenerate strategies discovered (or patched)
* [ ] Rulebook is self-sufficient for unsupervised play
