== Overview

The planetary boundaries framework (Stockholm Resilience Centre, Rockstrom et al.
2009, updated 2023) identifies nine Earth system processes that regulate planetary
stability. These boundaries define a "safe operating space" for humanity; crossing
them risks destabilising Earth's systems, potentially leading to irreversible
environmental damage.

As of 2023, *seven* of these boundaries have been crossed.

== The Nine Boundaries

[cols="2,1,3,2,3"]
|===
| Boundary | Status (2023) | Definition | Threshold | Game Impact

| *Climate Change*
| CROSSED
| Atmospheric CO2 concentration and radiative forcing
| 350 ppm CO2 (current: 423 ppm)
| +1 CO2 per factory built; crisis triggered at stress level 10

| *Biosphere Integrity (Genetic)*
| CROSSED
| Rate of species extinction
| <10 extinctions per million species-years (current: 100-1000x)
| -1 Food per 2 deforestations; biodiversity collapse cascade

| *Biosphere Integrity (Functional)*
| CROSSED
| Loss of ecosystem functional integrity
| >80% remaining (many biomes below)
| Ecosystem service degradation; Nature resource penalty

| *Biogeochemical Flows (Nitrogen)*
| CROSSED
| Industrial and agricultural fixation of N2
| 62 Tg N/yr (current: ~150 Tg N/yr)
| Agricultural runoff events; freshwater contamination cascade

| *Biogeochemical Flows (Phosphorus)*
| CROSSED
| P flow from fertilisers to erodible soils
| 6.2 Tg P/yr (current: ~22 Tg P/yr)
| Eutrophication events; dead zone penalties

| *Land System Change*
| CROSSED
| Area of forested land as % of original
| 75% forest remaining (current: ~60%)
| Territory expansion costs increase; habitat fragmentation

| *Freshwater Change*
| CROSSED
| Blue and green water cycle disruption
| Flow deviation from pre-industrial
| Water scarcity events; agricultural yield penalties

| *Novel Entities*
| CROSSED (2022)
| Chemical pollution, plastics, endocrine disruptors
| No single metric; systemic overload
| Health crises; cleanup costs; tech-dependent mitigation

| *Stratospheric Ozone Depletion*
| SAFE
| O3 concentration in stratosphere
| 275 DU (current: recovering)
| Historical event card only (Montreal Protocol success story)

| *Ocean Acidification*
| AT RISK
| Aragonite saturation state of surface ocean
| >80% pre-industrial (current: ~84%)
| Marine food chain disruption; fishing yield penalties

| *Atmospheric Aerosol Loading*
| REGIONAL
| Aerosol optical depth; regional variation
| No single global threshold
| Regional health events; visibility/agriculture penalties
|===

== Interdependence and Cascading Risk

Crossing one boundary shifts others. The game models these cascades:

* *Climate -> Freshwater*: Warming disrupts precipitation patterns
* *Land Change -> Biosphere*: Deforestation drives extinction
* *Biogeochemical -> Freshwater*: Nitrogen/phosphorus runoff contaminates water
* *Novel Entities -> Biosphere*: Chemical pollution harms biodiversity
* *Climate -> Ocean Acidification*: CO2 absorption lowers ocean pH

=== Cascade Mechanic

When a boundary reaches stress level 8+, linked boundaries gain +1 stress per
turn automatically. This creates positive feedback loops that can spiral into
collapse if left unaddressed.

== Planetary Boundaries Dashboard

The dashboard is a central game component visible to all players.

=== Dashboard Layout

* Nine boundary tracks (0-10 stress scale each)
* Colour zones: *Green* (0-3 safe), *Amber* (4-6 increasing risk), *Red* (7-9 danger), *Black* (10 — crossed, irreversible)
* Cascade arrows showing linked boundaries
* Global stress indicator (sum of all boundary stresses)

=== Loss Condition

If *four or more* boundaries reach Black (10), the game ends in *planetary collapse*
— all players lose regardless of individual progress.

=== Recovery Mechanics

* Green economic policies reduce stress by 1 per turn on targeted boundaries
* Techno-utopian breakthroughs can reset a single boundary by 3 (once per game)
* Cooperative agreements (eXchange pillar) allow joint stress reduction
* Some boundaries have hysteresis — recovery is slower than degradation
