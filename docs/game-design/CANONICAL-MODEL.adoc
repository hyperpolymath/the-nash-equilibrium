[.lead]
This is the *single source of truth* for the rules of The Nash Equilibrium. Every other
design document is subordinate to it; where any doc disagrees with this one, this one wins.
Variable content (card text, boundary thresholds, production recipes, victory numbers) lives
in machine-readable *data files* (see <<data>>), so the tabletop kit and any future digital
edition both *derive* from the same source and never drift apart.

NOTE: Status — *foundational draft, 2026-07*. Bakes in owner rulings DD-006, DD-008, DD-009,
DD-010. Open holes: DD-011, DD-012 (see <<open>>). Numeric thresholds marked _(provisional)_
are the primary playtest tuning knobs.

== Design philosophy (the guardrails)

These three principles constrain every mechanic, card, and subsystem. They are testable — if
a proposed rule fails them, it is cut or simplified.

[horizontal]
The corridor:: Deeper than roll-and-move (never *Monopoly*: no game where luck dominates
agency), but never a hyper-detailed simulation (never *Grigsby*: no lookup-table-per-turn
data entry). The strategic ceiling aspires to *Civilization*'s feel.
Emergence, not simulation:: Depth comes from a *few elegant systems interacting*, not from
exhaustive realistic detail. Easy to learn, hard to master.
The one filter:: For every rule, card, or token ask: *does this add a decision, or just
bookkeeping?* If it is bookkeeping, cut it or automate it.

== The scalability architecture

The game is *one ruleset with two independent dials*: **length** and **weight (complexity)**.
This is what lets it be a minimalist family game or a Civilization-depth epic without
becoming a different game.

=== Weight dial — modular layers (DD-008)

The game ships as a *complete minimalist Core* plus optional depth *layers*. The Core is a
real, satisfying game on its own — not a tutorial. Each layer adds exactly one strategic
*system* (never a pile of exceptions).

[cols="1,4,1",options="header"]
|===
| Layer | Adds | ~Weight
| *Core* | 5 resources · the **core-four boundaries** · simplified production · 2 actions/turn · basic crisis draw · open trades · pathway goals | 2.0
| *+ Boundaries* | full 9 boundaries + cascades + pollution→stress conversion | +0.4
| *+ Economy* | full Leontief recipe engine, externality tokens, factor-income model | +0.4
| *+ Diplomacy* | typed trade cards, binding treaties, the defection ledger _(the game-theory heart)_ | +0.4
| *+ Research* | the eXperiment d8 branching technology | +0.3
| *+ Legacy* | 5th player, expansion roles, megaprojects (Dyson Sphere), multi-session state | +0.4
|===

Minimalist = Core only (~2.0). Full "Civilization-depth" = Core + every layer (~4.0–4.5).

=== Length dial — presets

[cols="1,2,2,2",options="header"]
|===
| | *Short — "Skirmish"* | *Standard — "Ascent"* | *Extended — "Legacy"*
| Purpose | Playtest / family / classroom on-ramp | The default box experience | Enthusiast / epic / Kickstarter flagship
| Eras | 2 (Agrarian→Industrial) | 3 (→ Digital) | 4 (→ Post-Scarcity)
| Turns | ~5–6 | ~9–10 | 32 (multi-session)
| Time target | 60–90 min | 120–180 min | 4–6 hr / across sittings
| Default layers | Core (+ Diplomacy) | Core + Boundaries + Economy + Diplomacy | all layers
| Default weight | ~2.0–2.5 | *3.0–3.5* | ~4.0–4.5
|===

The presets are just *bundles of the two dials* — players may mix (e.g. a short-but-deep game).
Post-Scarcity is the narrative *horizon*; Short and Standard end at their final era with a
scored assessment rather than requiring era 4 to be fully played.

=== Leaner-turn levers

Applied to Short by default; offered as optional speed dials for Standard. They cut turn time
without changing the rules:

* *Simultaneous Production* — all players resolve production at once, not in sequence.
* *Time-boxed Exchange* — a fixed ~3-minute trading window per turn.
* *Batched boundary update* — resolve all pollution→stress and cascades in one end-of-turn pass.

== Core concepts

=== Resources (5)

Labour, Capital, Nature, Knowledge, Energy. In the *Economy layer* these map onto the classical
factor model:

[cols="1,1,2",options="header"]
|===
| Resource | Economic factor | Income form
| Nature | Land | Rent
| Labour | Labour | Wage
| Capital | Capital | Interest
| Knowledge | Entrepreneurship | Profit (Schumpeterian)
| Energy | _intermediate good — not a factor_ | (consumed in production)
|===

=== Planetary boundaries (9)

On a shared dashboard, each with a stress track *0–10*: **Climate Change · Biosphere Integrity ·
Freshwater Change · Novel Entities** (the *core-four*, used in the Core game) plus **Land-System
Change · Biogeochemical Flows (N & P) · Stratospheric Ozone Depletion · Ocean Acidification ·
Atmospheric Aerosol Loading** (added by the Boundaries layer).

[cols="1,2",options="header"]
|===
| Zone | Stress
| Green | 0–3
| Amber | 4–6
| Red | 7–9
| Black | 10
|===

=== Resource → boundary pressure

Exploiting a resource stresses linked boundaries (canonical map, Boundaries layer):

[horizontal]
Labour:: Freshwater
Capital:: Climate, Novel Entities
Nature:: Biosphere, Land-System
Knowledge:: Ozone, Novel Entities
Energy:: Climate, Aerosols

== Turn structure (7 phases)

RECONCILED: Phase 1 is *active Leontief Production*, superseding the stale "passive collect"
text in `core-mechanics.adoc` (see <<reconcile>>).

. *Production* — fire the Leontief recipes you can activate; gain outputs, emit externality tokens.
. *Action* — take 2 (Core) to 3 actions: eXplore, eXpand, eXploit, eXterminate (see <<pillars>>).
. *Exchange* — the eXchange pillar: trade resources / play typed trade cards (Diplomacy layer).
. *Experiment* — the eXperiment pillar: research via a d8 outcome table (Research layer).
. *Crisis* — draw *N* crisis cards for the era (N per <<crisis>>).
. *Boundary update* — convert pollution→stress, resolve cascades, update the dashboard.
. *Assessment* — check victory / loss conditions.

IMPORTANT: *Crisis-draw timing (reconciled).* The boundary stress that sets *N* is sampled at
*start of turn*, not at the start of the Crisis phase. This closes the "recovery-spam to dodge
this turn's crises" exploit flagged in `gameplay-storyboard.adoc`.

[[pillars]]
== The 6X pillars

The classic 4X plus two game-theoretic pillars, mapped to the turn:

[cols="1,3",options="header"]
|===
| Pillar | Where it lives
| eXplore | Action phase — reveal map / opportunities
| eXpand | Action phase — grow territory / capacity
| eXploit | Action + Production — extract resources (stresses boundaries)
| eXterminate | Action phase — competitive/coercive plays
| *eXchange* | Exchange phase — repeated-game trade & diplomacy; defection is possible and sometimes rational, but destabilises the shared dashboard
| *eXperiment* | Experiment phase — speculative research, branching non-deterministic payoffs
|===

== Production (Economy layer)

Binary-activation *Leontief* recipes: a recipe either has *all* its inputs (and fires, all-or-
nothing) or it does not. Chosen over Cobb–Douglas because cubes on cardboard cannot represent
fractional inputs. Firing a recipe emits *externality tokens* (pollution) that convert to
boundary stress in Phase 6 at *1 stress per 3 tokens* _(provisional)_. In the Core game,
production is simplified to fixed per-resource yields without the full recipe engine.

[[cascades]]
== Boundaries & cascades (Boundaries layer) — DD-010

A *cascade* is when an over-stressed boundary spills stress into the boundaries it is linked to,
modelling Earth-system tipping points. Canonical cascade links:

* Climate → Freshwater
* Climate → Ocean Acidification
* Land-System → Biosphere
* Biogeochemical → Freshwater
* Novel Entities → Biosphere

Ruling DD-010 _(all provisional — top of the playtest tuning list)_:

[horizontal]
Trigger:: A boundary cascades when its stress reaches *8* (entering the Red→Black zone).
Spill:: *+1* stress onto each linked boundary (*+2* in the Extended/Legacy layer).
Chaining:: *On, but capped* — a cascaded boundary may itself cascade, but each boundary
cascades at most *once per turn* (real runaway spirals without a single turn wiping the board).

*Collapse (all players lose):* *4 or more* of the 9 boundaries at Black (stress 10).

[[crisis]]
== Crisis / event deck

Era-stratified decks totalling *78* cards (Agrarian 12 / Industrial 20 / Digital 24 /
Post-Scarcity 22). Each turn the number drawn scales with planetary stress:

[source]
----
N = ceil( 1 + 0.5·(#amber) + 1·(#red) + 2·(#black) ),  clamped to [1, 5]
----

Cooperative stewardship (boundaries in Green/Amber) → fewer crises; exploitation (Red/Black) →
crisis cascades. This is the mechanical engine of the game's thesis.

== Player pathways (4) — DD-006 applied

Real living/near-living figures are replaced by *archetypes* that carry a subtle name/emblem
*nod* to the original, keeping them legally safe and instantly recognisable. Asymmetric
bonuses/constraints, balanced for interesting decisions rather than raw power.

[cols="1,1,3",options="header"]
|===
| Pathway | Archetype (nod) | Identity
| Capitalist | *The Financier-Sovereign* (Reina / Castella) | crown-and-coin sigil; patron-of-expeditions; rapid wealth extraction
| Socialist | *The Great-Soul Steward* (Shanti / Ananda) | spinning-wheel + salt emblem; cooperative, redistributive, self-sufficiency
| Green | *The Warden* (Gaia / Sylva) | leaf-and-hourglass; boundary protection, degrowth, doughnut economics
| Techno-Utopian | *The Prometheus* | rocket-and-flame; technology-as-solution, high-risk breakthroughs
|===

NOTE: Expansion roles (e.g. a *Revolutionary* archetype nodding to Marx) follow the same
archetype-with-a-nod rule and live in the Legacy layer.

== Victory & loss

=== The Nash mechanic

The winning position is never unilateral dominance — it is the *Nash equilibrium*: the state
no player can improve by defecting alone. Per the *Axiom of Emergent Stability*, this is
*emergent, not computed* — there is no stability index. It is read off the dashboard, the
defection ledger, the Labour floors, and the size of the next crisis draw.

=== Conditions

[horizontal]
Cooperative victory:: The shared megaproject (Dyson Sphere, Legacy layer) completed *and* all
boundaries Green/Amber *and* no collapse.
Pathway victories:: Each of the 4 pathways has its own individual win condition (numeric
thresholds _provisional_, tuned in playtest).
Loss:: Planetary collapse (4+ boundaries Black) · social collapse (a player's Labour = 0) ·
resource exhaustion (Nature = 0).
