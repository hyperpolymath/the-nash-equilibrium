= Gameplay Storyboard — Turn 12, Industrial Era
:toc:
:icons: font

== Purpose

A turn-by-turn walkthrough of one representative round, used to pressure-test
the seven-phase turn structure (see `core-mechanics.adoc`) against the dynamic
crisis-scaling formula and the four asymmetric pathways (`player-roles.adoc`).
The economic structure assumed throughout — four factors and their returns,
the Leontief production model, the Pigouvian/Ostromian dashboard, and the
Hayekian emergence axiom — is specified in `economic-foundations.adoc`; this
storyboard does not restate those commitments, it cites them.

This is *design fiction*: nothing here is canonical card text — but every
number is computed from the rules as currently written.

If a phase here feels awkward, slow, or unclear, that is a finding — the rules
need to change, not the storyboard.

== Scene-Setting

* *Players (4):* Isabella (Capitalist), Gandhi (Socialist), Musk (Techno-Utopian),
  Greta (Green Activist).
* *Era:* Industrial (turns 9–16). Turn 12 — three turns in.
* *Tension:* Climate Change crossed Amber→Red at the *end* of turn 11. Players
  are about to discover what that means for the crisis deck.

== Phase 1 — Production (recipe firings)

Implements the Leontief production model from `economic-foundations.adoc` §3
under the four-factor mapping in §4. Each player examines their recipe set;
for each recipe whose inputs are present, the player elects to fire (paying
input cubes, receiving output cubes, and emitting any externality token) or
to idle the recipe. *The set of unfired recipes marks the binding-scarcity
decisions of the turn* (`economic-foundations.adoc` §2.1).

Recipe templates active in the Industrial era (full library lives in
`core-mechanics.adoc`):

----
  RECIPE       INPUTS                  OUTPUTS              EXTERNALITY
  ───────────  ──────────────────────  ───────────────────  ─────────────
  Farm         1 Labour                2 Nature             —
  Factory      1 Labour, 1 Energy      2 Capital            +1 pollution
  Trade Post   1 Capital               1 Capital per        —
                                       linked player
  School       1 Labour                1 Knowledge          —
  Lab          1 Knowledge, 1 Energy   2 Knowledge          —
  Reserve      —                       1 Nature, -1 Land    —
                                       stress (per turn)
----

Pollution tokens accumulate on the player board and convert to Climate
stress in Phase 6 at a rate of 1 stress per 3 tokens (round down;
remainder carries to next turn). This is the externality channel
(`economic-foundations.adoc` §5).

Per-player firings (factor flows in cubes; externality in tokens):

----

    Factory ×3      pay 3 L, 3 E      → +6 Cap                ✓ all fire
    Trade Post #1   pay 1 Cap         → +2 Cap (colonial      ✓ fires
                                         route — pre-existing)

    Innovation role -1 Labour (automation displacement)        ✓

    Lab             pay 1 K, 1 E      → +2 K                  ✓

----

*Design check 1.1 (scarcity legibility).* The unfired recipes — Isabella's
Trade Post #2, Musk's Energy ceiling, Gandhi's Labour drawdown — *are* the
interesting decisions of Phase 1. The previous additive model concealed all
of them; this one surfaces them as physical idle/active tile states.

*Design check 1.2 (4X-pillar binding).* Per `economic-foundations.adoc` §8,
each recipe firing presupposes a history of eXplore/eXpand/eXperiment
actions that established the recipe template, the hex it operates on, and
the visibility of the surrounding biome. We do not re-narrate that history
per turn, but each physical recipe tile *must* display the 4X-pillar
preconditions on its face.

*Design check 1.3 (factor returns are implicit).* Rent, Wage, Profit, and
Interest are not paid as separate cube types; they are subsumed in the
input/output structure of the recipes (Rent is implicit in the hex
ownership underwriting Land inputs; Wage in the Labour cube paid; Interest
in the Capital cube discount; Profit accrues to the player as the residual
when a novel recipe earns more than competitive imitators). This is
Limitation 4 in `economic-foundations.adoc` §9.

== Phase 2 — Action (2–3 actions per player)

Played roughly simultaneously, resolved in turn order.

* *Isabella (3 actions):* (a) Build 1 Trade Post in Greta's territory — opens
  trade lane. (b) Spend 4 Cap on Boundary Recovery — reduces Climate stress by 1
  (mitigation tech). (c) Pay 2 Cap to draw a Trade Agreement card. *Capital: 9 → 1.*
* *Gandhi (2 actions):* (a) Build 1 School (+1 Lab/turn ongoing). (b) Convert
  1 Farm to Cooperative (raises poverty floor for whole table by 1). *Labour: 7 → 4.*
* *Musk (3 actions):* (a) Begin Research: "Solar Cell" (-3 K, resolves in
  Experiment phase). (b) Pay 2 Lab to suppress automation unrest. (c) Build 1 Lab.
  *Knowledge: 6 → 3, Labour: 3 → 1 (danger).*
* *Greta (2 actions):* (a) Restore Land boundary: spend 2 Nat → -1 Land stress.
  (b) Sign Trade Agreement card with Isabella (deferred to Exchange phase).
  *Nature: 7 → 5.*

----
  Actions taken this turn (icons):

  Isabella  [trade-post] [recover Climate -1] [draw card]
  Gandhi    [school]     [cooperative +floor]
  Musk      [research]   [suppress unrest]    [lab]
  Greta     [recover Land -1] [sign trade]

  Boundary changes from Action phase:
    Climate Change       7 → 6  (Isabella's mitigation)
    Land System Change   5 → 4  (Greta's restoration)
----

*Design check:* Climate dropping back to 6 *might* take it out of Red before
crisis draw — players will absolutely lawyer this. **Rule needed:** does the
crisis-card formula sample boundaries at *start* of the Crisis phase, or at the
*start of turn*? Recommend **start of turn**, otherwise the Action phase
becomes "spam recovery to dodge crises" which collapses the whole strategic
texture. *(File this as a design decision.)*

== Phase 3 — Exchange

Repeated-game trade and diplomacy. Trades are *typed*: each agreement is
a card drawn from the trade-card library (`trade-cards.adoc`), with named
template, defined parties, mechanical defection consequences, and a
mechanical/strategic cue line on the card face. Free-form bartering is not
supported by design.

=== Bilateral: Greta ↔ Isabella

*Card played:* `DEBT-FOR-NATURE SWAP`.

* *Party A (creditor — Isabella):* pledges 1 Capital per turn for 3 turns,
  plus 1 pollution-offset token per turn (cancels 1 of her Phase 1 pollution
  tokens before Phase 6 conversion).
* *Party B (debtor — Greta):* cedes 3 Nature now; commits 1 hex to permanent
  Reserve status (the hex contributes Reserve recipe yield to the table for
  the rest of the game).
* *Defection clauses* (printed on card):
** If Isabella misses a payment, Greta reclaims the Reserve hex and the
   *defection ledger* (see Design check 3.2) records Isabella's break.
** If Greta violates the Reserve, Isabella recovers all paid Capital and
   the defection ledger records Greta's break.
* *Tokens placed:* one signed-card token on the Greta–Isabella trade lane;
  one Reserve marker on the ceded hex; one obligation tracker on Isabella
  (3 turns).

=== Multilateral (proposed, failed): Gandhi's Coal Phase-Out Pact

* *Card played:* `COLLECTIVE EMISSIONS PACT`.
* *Terms proposed:* No player adds a new Factory recipe to their active set
  until turn 16. If upheld by all signatories at turn 16, table receives
  -2 Climate stress.
* *Quorum:* 3 of 4 signatories required for the pact to be *active*.
* *Outcome:*
** Isabella abstains (her economy is coal-dependent).
** Gandhi, Musk, Greta sign.
** Quorum (3/4) reached → pact *would* be active … but the card text
   specifies that *unanimous* multilateral pacts on Climate-class boundaries
   are required for table-wide stress reductions. Three-of-four pacts are
   *advisory* and produce no mechanical reduction.
** **Card moves to the *defection ledger* (face up, visible to all).**
   Isabella's abstention is recorded with her token.

----
  Exchange ledger (end of phase):

       Isabella  Gandhi  Musk   Greta
        ────────  ──────  ────   ─────
  DNS:    ●─────────────────────  ●     Debt-for-Nature Swap (active, 3 turns)
  CEP:    [ABS]  ○      ○        ○     Collective Emissions Pact (advisory)

  ●  signatory of an active agreement
  ○  signatory of a failed/advisory pact (recorded in defection ledger)
  [ABS]  abstained (recorded in defection ledger)
----

*Design check 3.1 (no historical flavour).* Trade cards carry mechanical
mnemonics or strategic cues, not historical analogues; historical context
lives in the rulebook appendix and the educational supplement
(`economic-foundations.adoc` §9, Limitation 7).

*Design check 3.2 (defection memory).* The defection ledger is a physical
zone on the board where all failed/advisory pacts and missed obligations
are placed face-up. Future trade cards may consult it (e.g.,
`MOST-FAVOURED-NATION` will not sign with a player carrying ≥2 ledger
tokens). This gives defection persistent mechanical memory across turns —
the property that was missing under free-form bartering.

*Design check 3.3 (information asymmetry preserved).* Pacts are signed in
the open, but trade cards may also be played *face-down* (closed
agreements; `economic-foundations.adoc` §6 on Akerlof-style asymmetric
information). Closed trades carry a small Stability cost when later
revealed, modelling the social cost of opacity.

== Phase 4 — Experiment

Speculative research; non-deterministic outcomes resolved on the d8.

* Musk's "Solar Cell" research. Roll d8:
** 1–2: *Failure.* Lose investment.
** 3–5: *Partial.* Tech unlocked at half effect.
** 6–7: *Success.* Tech unlocked, full effect.
** 8: *Breakthrough.* Tech unlocked + draw a Breakthrough card.
* Roll: *6.* Solar Cell unlocked at full effect.
** Effect: Musk's Factories generate Energy without Climate stress for the rest
   of the game. Open-source option available — would grant +1 Knowledge to every
   player but reduce Musk's pathway lead.
** Musk *keeps it private.* (Records this in pathway tracker — open-sourcing
   counts toward the *Digital Commons* cooperative ending.)

*Design check:* The keep/open-source decision is exactly the kind of
defection-at-equilibrium choice the README promises. **The breakthrough card
itself should make this choice mechanically prominent** — front-of-card text:
"Keep (private bonus) — Open-source (collective bonus + 1 Cooperative ending
progress)." The decision shouldn't be buried in a rulebook footnote.

== Phase 5 — Crisis

Draw 4 cards from the Industrial deck. Resolve in order.

----
  Card 1 ─ DUST BOWL                                    [Industrial / Environmental]
  Trigger: Land ≥ 4. (Land = 4. Triggers.)
  Effect : All players with Farm infrastructure: -1 Nature per Farm.
           +1 Land System Change. +1 Biosphere Integrity (cascade).
  Choice : Spend 2 Knowledge to "rotate crops" → halve effect for self.

  Resolutions:
    Gandhi (4 Farm): -4 Nature. Spends 2 K to rotate → -2 Nature instead.
    Greta  (2 Farm): -2 Nature. Has Reserve → cancels effect entirely.
    Isabella (0): unaffected.
    Musk (0): unaffected.

  Card 2 ─ COAL BOOM                                    [Industrial / Economic]
  Effect : Factory-heavy players gain +2 Cap per Factory but +1 Climate per
           Factory beyond the first.
  Resolutions:
    Isabella (3 Factory): +6 Cap, +2 Climate.
    Musk     (1 Factory): +2 Cap, +0 Climate.
    Gandhi/Greta: no Factory; unaffected.

  Card 3 ─ MONTREAL FORESHADOWING                       [Industrial / Tech (positive)]
  Effect : Knowledge leader may invest 3 K to pre-empt the Ozone boundary —
           Ozone gains +2 max-resistance for the rest of the game.
  Resolution:
    Musk is K-leader (post-Solar at 3 K) — declines (just spent 3 on Solar).
    Card discarded; effect lost.

  Card 4 ─ WORKERS' REVOLT                              [Industrial / Social]
  Trigger: Any player with Labour ≤ 2.
  Hits  : Musk (Labour 1).
  Effect: Musk loses 1 Factory OR concedes 2 Capital + 1 Stability to Gandhi
          (mediation) OR spends 2 Capital + 1 Knowledge to suppress.
  Choice: Musk concedes — pays Gandhi 2 Cap, +1 Stability to Gandhi.
          (Suppression would have raised Musk's pathway "instability" tracker.)

  ── Boundary changes from Crisis phase ────────────────
     Land System Change    4 → 5  (Dust Bowl)
     Biosphere Integrity   4 → 5  (Land cascade)
     Climate Change        6 → 8  (Coal Boom — back into Red, deeper)
----

*Design check:* This is the densest phase by far — four cards is plausibly
*too much* in real play time. **Recommend:** prototype with a "fast resolution"
template (one card = at most one die roll + one binary choice). If a card
needs more, it's a *Major Crisis* and is drawn instead of two regular cards.

== Phase 7 — Assessment

Check victory and loss conditions. Per `economic-foundations.adoc` §7
(Emergence Axiom), *no separate stability index is computed*. Stability is
read off the existing rule mechanisms — the boundary dashboard, the
defection ledger, the Labour floors, and the next-turn crisis draw.

* *Planetary Collapse?* No (Black boundaries: 0; need 4). **Read from the
  dashboard.**
* *Social Collapse?* Musk's Labour = 1 (after Phase 5 Workers' Revolt
  resolution where he conceded). Above 0. Survives. **Read from his player
  board.**
* *Resource Exhaustion?* Isabella's Nature = 0 + 3 (Debt-for-Nature swap
  this turn). Safe. **Read from her player board.**
* *Cooperative-ending progress* (read from the cooperative-ending tracker
  on the main board, not computed):
** Digital Commons: 0/3 breakthroughs open-sourced (Musk declined to
   open-source Solar this turn).
** Harmonic Degrowth: blocked while Climate ≥ Red.
** Post-Scarcity Utopia: blocked (no megaproject infrastructure yet).
** Managed Decline: live but distant (no boundaries at Black yet).
* *Pathway leaders* (read from each player's pathway-progress tracker):
** Capitalist: Isabella (highest Capital among Capital pathways; one active
   trade route — needs routes to *all* players).
** Socialist: Gandhi (highest Stability; one cooperative built).
** Green: Greta (one boundary recovered this turn; high Nature).
** Techno-Utopian: Musk (one breakthrough; Solar held private).

*Stability signals at end of turn 12 (read, not calculated):*

* Boundary dashboard: 1 Red, 4 Amber, 4 Green — *worsening since last turn*.
* Defection ledger: 1 advisory pact (CEP); 0 active defections.
* Labour floors: Musk at 1 (one Workers' Revolt away from elimination);
  others ≥ 4.
* Next-turn crisis draw: 4 cards — *unchanged from this turn, no relief*.

These four signals together are what the table reads to know whether the
game is in equilibrium, drifting, or collapsing. No statistic combines them.

*Turn ends.* Turn marker advances to 13. Era still Industrial.

== Reading the Storyboard as a Design Tool

Findings surfaced by walking this single turn:

[cols="1,4"]
|===
| # | Finding

| 1
| The crisis-scaling formula needs a *sampling time* rule. Sampling at start of
  turn keeps strategy honest; sampling at start of Crisis phase incentivises
  recovery-spam. (See Phase 2 design check.)

| 2
| Failed pacts need persistent visibility. A discarded-pacts area on the board
  gives defection mechanical memory across turns. (See Phase 3.)

| 3
| Breakthrough cards must surface the keep/open-source decision *on the card
  face*, not in the rulebook. (See Phase 4.)

| 4
| Four crisis cards in a single Crisis phase is heavy. Either time-box card
  resolution or introduce a "Major Crisis" that costs two card slots.
  (See Phase 5.)

| 5
| Player-board ASCII (above) suggests the physical player boards should show
  resource pools, infrastructure, hand, *and pathway-progress trackers* — the
  four pathway-victory conditions need persistent in-game visibility, not a
  rulebook lookup. (Implicit throughout.)

| 6
| The Industrial-era turn is dense: ~7 phases × 4 players × 4 crisis cards.
  *Estimated wall-clock for turn 12: 25–35 minutes.* If the game is 32 turns,
  that does not fit a single sitting. The era-counts may need to compress, or
  later eras need to be lighter, not heavier.

| 7
| Stability is *emergent*, not measured (Hayek 1945; codified as the
  Emergence Axiom in `economic-foundations.adoc` §7). No stability index,
  harmony score, or harmonic-mean rating phase is to be introduced. The
  signal lives in the dashboard, defection ledger, Labour floors, and
  next-turn crisis draw — read off the board, not computed.

| 8
| Trade agreements must be *typed and named*, not free-form. Each trade is
  a card from the trade-card library (`trade-cards.adoc`) with explicit
  parties, terms, defection clauses, and a printed strategic cue. Free-form
  bartering is removed from the design.

| 9
| Recipe firings must *bind* to 4X-pillar action history (eXplore, eXpand,
  eXperiment) per `economic-foundations.adoc` §8. A recipe is not freestanding
  furniture on the player board; it is a contract between an action history
  and a production output. Physical recipe tiles must display 4X-pillar
  precondition icons on their face.
|===

== Next Storyboards (suggested)

To complete the design coverage, follow up with:

. *Turn 4 Agrarian* — verifies the "low pressure / 1 card" baseline feels
  *different enough* to be a distinct era, not just slow Industrial.
. *Turn 18 Digital* — first appearance of AI Uprising and breakthrough chains;
  exercises Novel Entities boundary.
. *Turn 28 Post-Scarcity, cooperative ending attempt* — the Dyson Sphere
  megaproject in active construction; verifies the cooperative-ending math.
. *Turn 28 Post-Scarcity, cascade-collapse path* — the same turn but with
  4 boundaries at Black; verifies the loss state actually feels like loss
  rather than a stalemate.

The *deltas* between these four storyboards are the design's load-bearing arc.
If they all feel similar, the era progression isn't pulling its weight.
